That sinking feeling
It’s been a long year for the Giants
By Mike Massa
July 11, 2017
<% var data = csv.winning_percentage %>

A year ago at the All-Star break, the Giants had the best record in Major League Baseball at 57-33, and fans were dreaming of another even-year parade through downtown San Francisco. But the past 12 months haven’t been kind. The team slumped in the second half of 2016, held on to make the playoffs as a wild-card team, and have been firmly planted in last place in the National League West for much of 2017.

In 162 games played from last year’s All-Star break to this year’s break — the equivalent of a full regular season — the Giants have gone 64-98. They set a franchise record with 56 losses before the break, and currently own the second-worst record in baseball.

A look inside the numbers shows the fall is no fluke. The following charts break down team stats based on pre-All-Star break and post-All-Star break numbers from 2016 and 2017.

Winning percentage
The Giants have gone from 102-win pace at the break last year to losing 98 of their next 162 games.
Runs per game
If it seems like the offense has gotten worse, it’s because it has — by nearly a full run per game.
Batting average
Imagine if Buster Posey was slumping like the rest of the team.
Home runs per game
Yes, the Giants are last in the league in homers, but they weren’t any better in the first half last year when they were winning.
OPS
Like runs per game, on-base plus slugging percentage is another stat that illustrates the offense’s dramatic decline.
Team ERA
Starting pitching is supposed to be the Giants’ strength. But when the starting rotation has a combined earned run average around 5.00 and the offense scores less than four runs per game, it’s no wonder the Giants are at the bottom of the standings.
